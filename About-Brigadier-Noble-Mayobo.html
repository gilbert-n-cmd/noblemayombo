Brigadier Noble Mayombo (1965â€“2007)

Early Life & Education

Born on April 9, 1965, in Kabarole District, Western Uganda, Brigadier Noble Mayombo hailed from the Babiito royal clan of the Tooro Kingdom. He was the seventh of twelve children in a family led by Rev. Canon James Rwabwoni and Beatrice Rwaboni Abwooli.

He attended Nyakasura School and Ntare School before joining Makerere University in 1985 to study law. He left to join the National Resistance Army (NRA) but later returned to complete his Bachelor of Laws (LLB), a Diploma in Legal Practice from the Law Development Centre, and a Master of Laws (LLM) in Human Rights Law.

Military & Public Service Career

  • 1986: Joined the NRA as an intelligence officer.
  • 1994: Youngest delegate to the Constituent Assembly that drafted the 1995 Constitution.
  • 1996â€“1997: Aide-de-Camp to President Yoweri Museveni.
  • 2000â€“2004: Chief of Military Intelligence.
  • 2005: Promoted to Brigadier and appointed Permanent Secretary in the Ministry of Defence.
  • 2005: Chairman of the Board, New Vision Group.

Mayombo was known for his intellect, discipline, and dedication. He played a key role in strengthening Uganda's military intelligence framework. His career was also marked by some controversy related to "safe houses," which he denied.

Illness & Death

On April 26, 2007, Mayombo fell ill after meetings at the Ministry of Defence. He was first admitted to Kololo Hospital, then transferred to International Hospital Kampala, and later to Aga Khan Hospital in Nairobi, where he died on May 1, 2007, at age 42, from acute pancreatitis.

President Museveni ordered an investigation into his sudden death. A team submitted a report in November 2007, but as of 2015, it had not been made public. His death remains a subject of speculation.

Legacy & Tributes

Mayombo was honored with a state funeral and buried at his ancestral home in Kijura, Kabarole District. The Mayombo Open Golf Tournament is held annually in Fort Portal in his memory. He was praised by President Museveni as a brilliant and incorruptible officer with great leadership potential.

Family

He is survived by his wife Juliet Mayombo and six children: Charlene Komuntale, Samora Olimi, Isabelle Byanjeru, Natalie Kabasweka, Nicole Komukyeya, and Kamurasi Noble Mayombo II.
